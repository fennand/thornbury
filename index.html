Alan Kitchin - Non-Executive Director

Alan brings an immense plethora of knowledge and expertise to his role as Non-Executive Director. He studied law initially at the British Institute in Florence and then at Cambridge University (Gonville and Caius College), where he won the Senior Squire Scholarship in Law. On graduating, he was awarded the Tapp Postgraduate Scholarship in Law, and he has lectured at Standford University with the American Law Institute on International Trade. Alan became a Christian while at Cambrdige University, and now both supports and volunteers with the charity Christian Aid.

Alan first qualified as a foreign lawyer in Japan in 1991, and was also the Chairman of the Law Asia Committee on Energy. Alan started working in New York with the international law firm Freshfields Bruckhaus Deringer, however later moved back to London to join Ashurst as a partner. This saw Alan in this post for twenty four years, before becoming the Managing Partner of its Asia practice. He is currently a Managing Partner of Simpson Kitchin LLP, and is a solicitor of the Supreme Court of England and Wales.
